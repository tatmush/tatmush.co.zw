Aaron Swartz and Me

The story was first introduced to me in 2017 I suppose, by one of my good teachers in university. We were studying Cyber Laws and Ethics course which I never knew was a discipline. We read the story and watched the documentaries as a class exercise, so i really had no option. Unbeknown to me the story stuck.

Today I decide to tell my version. It reads like a tale. Its striking and saddening.
I do not understand it. There is a love of Aaron that just turns in me. Its common to romanticize the dead, especially if you are shona, nothing suprising here.

Maybe its the nature of writing and not the story, but i severely doubt. It has to be the story. It is the story.
I will do little to no effort in narration of this great movie story but rather draw from a well stirred within. You can read his story here (a recent version i have read) -> Requiem for a Dream. I would not recommend starting with that one though. It is for us who have already fallen in love and heart broken.

Unfinished projects

The fact that Aaron would start and not finish a project resonates with me. It is common among developers to start projects and leave them because interest has peaked. I have done just about a bit of everything and really have not pursued to be in the top 10%. I'm only skilled and experienced enough to learn more from an expert and ask the right questions or give guidance to an inquisitor. But with that, sometimes i struggle to really discover what i really want and end up digging a mile wide and an inch deep. Weather this is good or bad i do not know.

Blogger

I remember my first attempt at blogging at around 11 or 12. I was graced enough to encounter a computer at a tender age by Zimbabwe standards. Clutching the innocence of a kid, i poured out my heart in writing into that microsoft word document. I did not really know what blogging was, but i knew that somewhere out there people were writing their experiences and sharing their lives.
In my novice adventures I penned one, only to find nasty comments at the bottom from my Dad. I know he felt sadistic in that moment. But he isn't one, the opposite rather.

I deleted it.
He discouraged me.
I never bothered again.
Until i was around 15 and we were discussing a blogging idea with a high school friend, Benson, over facebook i think. He was a bright kid. We all were.
That dream didn't materialize.
There were several deaths and resurrections and now is another.
I guess you can do what you are only meant to do.

Annoyed by Computers

I think I wrote somewhere about my academic journey. How I never knew IT as a discipline until 2015.
2020 I graduated.
2021, hitherto I loved IT.
Coding and hacking and WiFi was life. Now? Life is something else, another me would argue money but I shall not entertain the thought for my Christian person.

I realize that technology is a part that enables and effects change.
Social skill is another.
Currently, I'd rather learn to sell, market, talk to people to convince than learn Go. I'd rather learn how to manage than discovering a Zero-Day.
Although it would be nice, but it wouldn't give as much satisfaction.
Technology is a part. Social skill is a part. It is a great realization that draws me towards learning anthropology or psychology.

A mile wide, inch deep hole.
